Women in Islam, the Kingdom of God vs. the Caliphate, and Islamic Violence

Speaker: Jay Smith

Core Thesis: Islam's treatment of women, its political vision of the caliphate, and its commands to violence stand in stark contrast to the Kingdom of God as described by Jesus. The Bible elevates women, defines God's kingdom as relational rather than territorial, and uses spiritual rather than physical weapons.

PART 1: Women in Islam

I. Seven Quran Verses on Women

1. Four wives permitted:

  • Surah 4:3 — "Marry women of your choice, two or three or four"
  • Muhammad himself had 11 wives simultaneously (exempted by Surah 33:50)

2. Half the inheritance:

  • Surah 4:11 — "The male shall have the equal of the portion of two females"
  • A daughter receives half of what a son receives

3. Concubines ("right hand possesses"):

  • Surah 4:24 — In addition to wives, men may take concubines from among captive women
  • The phrase ma malakat aymanukum ("what your right hand possesses") appears repeatedly in the Quran to authorize sexual access to slave women

4. Beating wives — three stages:

  • Surah 4:34 — "As to those women on whose part you see ill-conduct: (1) admonish them, (2) refuse to share their beds, (3) beat them"
  • This is a sequential escalation: verbal warning, then withholding intimacy, then physical striking
  • Modern apologists claim "beat lightly" or "tap with a toothbrush" — the Arabic idribuhunna means "strike them"

5. A woman's testimony is worth half a man's:

  • Surah 2:282 — "Get two witnesses out of your own men, and if there are not two men, then a man and two women"
  • Two women required to equal one man's testimony in court

6. Wife as a field to be plowed:

  • Surah 2:223 — "Your wives are as a tilth [field] unto you; so approach your tilth when or how you will"
  • The wife is compared to agricultural land — the husband's property to use as he wishes

7. Triple talaq divorce:

  • Surah 2:230 — After a man divorces his wife three times, she cannot return to him until she has married another man and been divorced by him
  • A man can divorce his wife simply by saying "I divorce you" three times — she has no equivalent right
  • The requirement to marry and divorce another man first (nikah halala) has been widely condemned as degrading

II. Muhammad and Aisha

  • Muhammad married Aisha when she was 6-7 years old
  • The marriage was consummated when she was 9 — Muhammad was 53
  • Recorded in Sahih al-Bukhari (Hadith 5:58:236) — the most authoritative hadith collection in Sunni Islam
  • Aisha herself narrated this account

III. The Bible's Contrast

  • Luke 10:38-42 — Mary sat at Jesus' feet as a disciple (a radical act in that culture — rabbis did not teach women)
  • Galatians 3:28 — "There is neither male nor female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus"
  • Ephesians 5:25 — "Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it" — sacrificial love, not authority to beat
  • The resurrection was first revealed to women (Matthew 28:1-10, John 20:11-18) — in a culture where women's testimony was not accepted in court, God chose women as the first witnesses of the most important event in history
  • Jesus never married, never took concubines, never struck a woman

PART 2: The Kingdom of God vs. the Caliphate

I. The Debate with Sheikh Omar Bakri Muhammad

  • Jay Smith debated Sheikh Omar Bakri Muhammad on the question: "Which is better for Britain — the Kingdom of God or the caliphate?"
  • The Sheikh wanted Sharia law implemented in the UK
  • The fundamental difference: one is a relationship, the other is a political state

II. The Kingdom of God

  • Matthew 18:20 — "Where two or three are gathered in my name, there am I in the midst of them"
  • The Kingdom is not a territory — it has no borders, no capital, no army
  • It is a relationship with God through Christ
  • It grows under persecution — the church has always expanded when oppressed
  • It cannot be destroyed by any government because it doesn't depend on any government
  • 2 Corinthians 10:4-5 — "For the weapons of our warfare are not carnal, but mighty through God to the pulling down of strongholds, casting down imaginations"
  • Our weapons are spiritual: prayer, truth, love, sacrifice — not swords, not armies

III. The Caliphate

The caliphate requires territorial control enforced by Sharia law:

  • Surah 33:59 — Women must be covered (hijab/niqab mandate)
  • Surah 4:34 — Wives may be beaten
  • Surah 5:38 — Thieves: "Cut off their hands"
  • Surah 24:2 — Fornication: "Flog each of them with a hundred stripes"
  • The caliphate depends on political power — when the state falls, the system collapses

IV. The "Noon" Sign and Modern Persecution

  • In 2014, ISIS marked Christian homes in Mosul, Iraq with the Arabic letter nun (noon) — standing for Nasrani (Nazarene/Christian)
  • Christians were given a choice: convert to Islam, pay the jizya tax, leave, or die
  • Over 100,000 Christians fled Mosul — a community that had existed since the 1st century
  • Christians worldwide adopted the nun symbol as a badge of solidarity
  • The persecution proved the point: the Kingdom of God survived the destruction of every building and institution, because it lives in people, not structures

PART 3: Islam, Submission, and Violence

I. Islam Means "Submission," Not "Peace"

  • The Arabic root s-l-m has multiple forms
  • 1st form: salama/salam — "peace" (used in greetings)
  • 4th form: aslama/islam — "submission, obedience, surrender"
  • A muslim is "one who submits" — not "one who is at peace"
  • These are different verb forms with different meanings — conflating them is a linguistic error
  • There is no verse in the Quran that commands Muslims to live at peace with non-Muslims

II. Violence Verses in the Quran

  • Surah 9:5 — "Slay the unbelievers wherever you find them, besiege them, lay in wait for them with every kind of ambush"
  • Surah 9:29 — "Fight against those who believe not in Allah... until they pay the jizya with willing submission and feel themselves subdued"
  • Surah 8:39 — "Fight them until there is no more fitnah [unbelief] and all religion belongs to Allah"
  • Surah 8:60 — "Prepare against them whatever you are able of power and steeds of war by which you may terrify the enemy of Allah"
  • Surah 5:51 — "O you who believe, do not take the Jews and Christians as allies"
  • Surah 47:1-4 — "When you meet the unbelievers, strike at their necks"

Surah 9 is the last major surah revealed — in Islamic theology, later revelations abrogate (cancel) earlier, more peaceful ones (naskh — the doctrine of abrogation).

III. Huntington's "Clash of Civilizations"

  • Samuel Huntington's 1996 thesis: future conflicts would be civilizational, not ideological
  • He coined the phrase "bloody borders" — noting that Islam's borders with other civilizations are disproportionately violent
  • Of 32 armed conflicts in the early 2000s, 28 involved Muslim-majority nations or groups
  • This is not about individual Muslims (most of whom want peace) but about the texts and traditions that authorize violence
