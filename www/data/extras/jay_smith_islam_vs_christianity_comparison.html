Islam vs. Christianity: A Side-by-Side Comparison

Speaker: Jay Smith | Interactive Teaching Format

Core Thesis: Starting from Genesis 3:8-9, we can show that Islam and Christianity differ on every fundamental point: who God is, what He has done, how He relates to us, and what awaits us. These two verses — God walking in the Garden and calling "Where are you?" — reveal everything Muslims need to hear.

The Key Text: Genesis 3:8-9

"Then the man and his wife heard the sound of the Lord God as he was walking in the garden in the cool of the day. And they hid from the Lord God among the trees of the garden. But the Lord God called to the man, 'Where are you?'"

These two verses reveal: who God is, who we are, what it was once like, what went wrong, and what needs to be rectified.

IV. The Prophetic Line and the Messiah

Genesis 3:15 — God tells Eve: from your line, a male will come who will crush the head of Satan (but Satan will bruise his heel — the crucifixion).

The Bible progressively reveals who this person is:

  • The Son of God — definite article, not "a" son
  • The Messiah — the anointed one, anointed to save. Only God can save.
  • The Son of Man — Daniel 7:14: "from everlasting to everlasting, dominion over all tribes, nations, peoples, and tongues" — that's God
  • Emmanuel — Isaiah 7:14: "A virgin will conceive and bear a son, and he shall be called Emmanuel — God with us"

The Quran confirms key pieces:

  • Surah 19:20 — Virgin Mary conceives Isa
  • Isa is called Al-Masih (the Messiah) 11 times — only he has this title
  • Surah 10:94 and 21:7 — "If you have any questions, go to the People of the Book"

Jesus' three divine claims before the Sanhedrin (Matthew 26:62-66):

  1. "Are you the Son of God?" — "It is as you say" (accepted the title)
  2. "Are you the Messiah?" — "It is as you say" (accepted the title)
  3. "You will see the Son of Man coming in the clouds" — claimed Daniel 7:14 for himself

Caiaphas tore his robe: "This man has blasphemed!" — three divine claims in one answer.

The greatest name: John 8:58 — "Before Abraham was, Yahweh." God's personal name from Exodus 3:14, used 6,823 times in the OT. God said: "This shall be my name forever, from generation to generation." Yet Muhammad never knew this name — it appears nowhere in the Quran, Hadith, Sira, or any Islamic tradition.

Three names for God in the Old Testament:

  • Adonai (descriptive) — ~300 times
  • Elohim (generic plural: three or more) — ~2,400 times. Closest to "Allah" but plural, not singular.
  • Yahweh (personal name) — 6,823 times. The most important name, so holy Jews won't pronounce it.

Practical Summary — What to Say to a Muslim:

  1. Start with Genesis 3:8-9 — God was here, walking with us, face to face
  2. Ask: "Can your God do that?" — the answer reveals everything
  3. Use Surah 20:12-14 — their own Quran shows Allah in the burning bush
  4. Point to the virgin birth — Isaiah 7:14 and Surah 19:20
  5. Show Jesus' divine claims — Matthew 26, John 8:58
  6. Ask: "What is God's name?" — Yahweh, 6,823 times. Muhammad never knew it.
  7. Contrast slave vs. child — "We call God Daddy. What do you call Him?"
  8. The Prodigal Son — our God runs to embrace the returning child
