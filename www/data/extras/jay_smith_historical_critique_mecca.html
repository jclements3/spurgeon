A Historical Critique of Islam's Origins: The Problem with Mecca

Speaker: Jay Smith | Location: Calvary Chapel Chino Hills

Core Thesis: Using the same historical-critical methods (textual, source, form, and redactive criticism) that were applied to the Bible in the 1800s — and which Christianity has since answered — we can examine Islam's foundational claims about the Quran, Muhammad, and Mecca. The evidence shows that Mecca did not exist in the 7th century, and without Mecca, both Muhammad and the Quran lose their foundation.

I. The Problem with Islamic Sources

Islam depends on three foundations: the Quran (the book), Muhammad (the man), and Mecca (the place). All three are claimed to originate in the 7th century. But the sources describing them are far too late:

Biographies of Muhammad (Sira):

  • Ibn Ishaq (d. 765) — 130 years after Muhammad. Nothing survives from him.
  • Ibn Hisham (d. 833) — 200 years after Muhammad. First surviving biography.
  • Al-Waqidi (d. 835) — also 200+ years late.

Sayings of Muhammad (Hadith):

  • Al-Bukhari (d. 870) — 240 years after Muhammad. Given 600,000 stories, kept only 7,397 (2%), discarded 98%.
  • Sahih Muslim (d. 884), Abu Dawud (d. 899), An-Nisa'i (d. 915) — all 240+ years late.

Commentaries (Tafsir) and Histories (Tarikh):

  • Al-Tabari (d. 923) — first to write both genres. 290+ years late.

Geographic problem: None of these writers lived in Mecca or Medina. Ibn Hisham wrote in Baghdad (1,200 miles away). Al-Bukhari came from Bukhara, Uzbekistan (2,600 miles away). All were Abbasid-era writers producing the Abbasid narrative.

The real biography: The standard "Life of Muhammad" used today was actually compiled by Heinrich Ferdinand Wustenfeld, a German linguist, between 1858-1860 from Ottoman-era manuscripts — only 160 years old, over 1,000 years too late.

Comparison with Christianity:

  • Acts of the Apostles: 20-30 years after Christ (52-62 AD)
  • Paul's letters: 15-34 years after Christ (48-65 AD)
  • Gospel of Mark: 37 years after Christ (70 AD)
  • Matthew and Luke: 47 years after Christ (80 AD)
  • Gospel of John: 57 years after Christ (90 AD)
  • All within 60 years, all written by people who lived where Jesus lived
  • If Christianity's sources were as late as Islam's, Jesus wouldn't appear until the 3rd century

II. The Problem with Mecca

Islamic claims about Mecca:

  • Oldest city in history (Adam and Eve sent there)
  • Abraham lived there and built the Ka'ba
  • Center of trade north, south, east, and west
  • Muhammad born there in 570 AD
  • Direction of all prayer (qibla) since 624 AD

What the evidence shows:

1. No documentation:

  • Patricia Crone (read/wrote 15 languages) searched every surrounding empire's records
  • Assyrians (Shalmaneser, Tiglath-Pileser, Sennacherib, Sargon) — no mention
  • Babylonians (Nabonidus) — no mention
  • Romans (Pliny the Elder, Ptolemy, Procopius, Strabo) — no mention
  • Persians — no mention
  • Nabataeans, Sabeans, Himyarites, Lakhmids, Kindites — no mention
  • Nubians, Axumites, Abyssinians — no mention
  • Earliest documentary reference: 741 AD (Apocalypse of Pseudo-Methodius) — over 100 years after Muhammad
  • Earliest map showing Mecca: 900 AD

2. No maps:

  • Ptolemy's 2nd-century geography of Arabia — no Mecca
  • Leonhart Holle (1482 map from Ptolemy) — no Mecca
  • Lorenz Fries (16th century) — no Mecca
  • Sebastian Munster (1571) — no Mecca
  • Yet lesser towns like Najran, Sana'a, Ta'if, Yathrib, Petra, Ma'rib are all well-documented

3. No archaeology:

  • Massive construction in Mecca (4th tallest building in the world, 62 skyscrapers) requires deep foundations
  • Saudi archaeologists found nothing prior to the 8th century
  • Oldest find: ruins of an Ottoman fort (1300 AD)
  • Claims of 70-300 prophets buried there — no remains found
  • Saudi Arabia is cementing over historical sites

4. No water, no life:

  • Soil studies confirm vegetation has never been supported there
  • No water → no food → no people → no towns → no cities → no civilization → no history
  • The Quran's own traditions describe the Prophet's city with streams, trees, grass, fruit, olive trees, fields — none of which exist in Mecca

5. The Zamzam Well debunked:

  • Only 30 feet wide, earliest documentation from the 1800s
  • Produces only 19 liters/second (after rain)
  • Mecca needs 1,481 liters/second for its 2 million residents
  • 98% of Mecca's water comes from desalination plants built by Bechtel (USA), Atkins (UK), and companies from Canada and Spain
  • The well has pipes running in and out — water is pumped in from desalination plants
  • Queen Zubaida's aqueduct (built ~801 AD) had to be refurbished 9 times before being replaced by desalination in 1926
  • The well is only 21 meters from the Ka'ba — Hagar's desert search story makes no sense at that distance

6. Trade route impossible:

  • Patricia Crone debunked the trade route theory in Meccan Trade and the Rise of Islam (1987)
  • Mecca sits 3,000 feet below the western plateau where the actual trade route runs
  • No reason to detour down to a waterless city and back up
  • Maritime trade via the Red Sea was 25x cheaper than overland
  • Ships hugged the African (western) coast, which had ports, fresh water, and populations
  • Jeddah (Mecca's port city) has no historical record before the 8th century
  • All trading documents show goods went via the African coast to Adulis (Eritrea), up to Petra, then to Gaza

7. Qibla evidence:

  • Dan Gibson surveyed 100+ early mosques using GPS (1-2 degree accuracy)
  • Every mosque built before 706 AD faces Petra, not Mecca
  • First mosque facing Mecca: 715 AD — almost 100 years after Muhammad

8. Quranic Arabic is from the north:

  • The Quran uses Nabataean Aramaic linguistic features (ta marbuta, alif maqsura, definite article "al")
  • Nabataean Aramaic originated in present-day Jordan — 600 miles north of Mecca
  • Mecca/Medina would have used Sabaean Arabic (from Yemen), which lacks these features

9. An inscription dates Mecca's construction:

  • A rock inscription found near Ta'if in Nabataean Aramaic reads: "This was written in the year the Masjid al-Haram was built in the 78th year"
  • 78 years after 622 AD = 697-698 AD
  • The word is "built," not "rebuilt" — Mecca was constructed during Abd al-Malik's reign in the late 7th century

III. Borrowed Origins of the Hajj

The pilgrimage rituals trace back to Jewish practices in Jerusalem, not Mecca:

  • Ka'ba (Arabic/Hebrew: "cube") — borrowed from the Holy of Holies in Solomon's Temple
  • Circumambulation 7 times counterclockwise — borrowed from the march around Jericho (Joshua 6)
  • Safa and Marwa — the "mountains" in Mecca are only 20 feet high. The originals are Mount Moriah (Marwa in Arabic) and Mount Scopus (Safa in Arabic) in Jerusalem
  • Zamzam Well — borrowed from the Pool of Siloam in Jerusalem
  • The Black Stone — a Phoenician meteorite (3rd-4th century BC), brought to Damascus by Roman Emperor Elagabalus, then to Petra by the Umayyads, then sent to Mecca by Ibn al-Zubayr in 685 AD. Kissing a stone for forgiveness contradicts Islamic monotheism.
  • Jamarat (stoning pillars) — Originally one pillar representing Satan. Rebuilt in 1980 as three structures. Narrative changed from "stoning the devil" to "stoning the temptations of Abraham" — the story was altered in our lifetime.

Quran references for "ask the People of the Book": Surah 10:94, Surah 21:7

IV. Key Historical Figures

  • Abd al-Malik (caliph 685-705) — First to put Muhammad's name on coins, the Dome of the Rock, and protocols. An anti-Trinitarian Christian, not a Muslim. 60 years after Muhammad supposedly died.
  • Mu'awiya (caliph 661-680) — Created the first Umayyad caliphate in Damascus (not Medina). Ruled from a Christian city.
  • Patricia Crone — Cambridge scholar, read 15 languages. Wrote Hagarism and Meccan Trade and the Rise of Islam. Received death threats at Oxford, had to move to Cambridge.
  • Dan Gibson — Archaeologist who discovered all early qibla directions point to Petra.

V. Violent Commands in the Quran

  • Surah 9:5 — "Slay the unbeliever wherever you find them, besiege them, lay in wait for them with every kind of ambush"
  • Surah 9:29 — "Make war on the People of the Book" (Jews and Christians)
  • Surah 8:60 — "Use any steeds of war to cause terror in the heart"
  • Surah 8:39 — "Slay the unbeliever until there is no more unbelief and all believe in Allah"
  • Surah 47:1-4 — Defines believers/unbelievers, then says "cut off the heads of the unbelievers"
  • Surah 5:33 — "For those who do not follow Allah or his Prophet, crucify them and cut off their hands and feet from opposite ends"

Scholarly Conclusions:

"Islam as we know it did not exist in the 7th century but more than likely evolved over a period of 200-300 years. The Quran probably was not revealed to one man in 22 years but likely evolved over a period of 50-100 years. The history of Islam, at least from the time of the Caliph Abd al-Malik (685-705) and before, is a later fabrication."

Final Conclusion: If Mecca did not exist in the 7th century, then Muhammad could not have lived there and the Quran could not have been revealed there. Without the place, the man and the book lose their foundation. Christians have the only alternative — a historically verified faith with eyewitness accounts within 60 years, a well-documented city (Jerusalem), and a risen Savior.
