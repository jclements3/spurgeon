FAQs: Defending Jesus & Confronting the Quran

Speaker: Jay Smith | Location: Calvary Chapel (Evening Session)

Core Thesis: When Muslims challenge you, don't use analogies for the Trinity — use Scripture. The questions Muslims ask about Jesus and the Bible can be answered directly from the Bible, and even from the Quran itself. Meanwhile, the Quran's own manuscript history shows it is far younger and more corrupted than Muslims claim.

PART 1: Answering Muslim Questions About Jesus

I. The Trinity

  • Don't use analogies — water/steam/ice, father/brother/son are all heresies. Muslims will demolish them.
  • Don't look for the word "Trinity" — it's not in the Bible. But "Tawheed" (Islamic oneness) isn't in the Quran either. Both are theological concepts.
  • The term "Trinity" was coined by Tertullian (~180 AD) to describe what Scripture reveals.
  • Best approach: Use Allah's own names against the Islamic position:
    • Surah 1:1 — al-Rahman (the compassionate), al-Rahim (the merciful)
    • Name #47: al-Wadud (the loving one)
    • These are eternal names. Compassion, mercy, and love require an object.
    • Who was Allah compassionate toward before creation? A monad cannot be relational.
    • We are relational because we're made in God's image — Genesis 1:26-27
    • The Triune God has always been relational: Father loves Son, Son loves Spirit

II. "How Could God Have a Son?"

  • Muslims think Christians claim God had sex with Mary — from Surah 5:116 (Allah asks Jesus: "Did you tell people to worship you and your mother as gods?")
  • This is a straw man — no evangelical teaches Mary is part of the Trinity. Possibly borrowed from the Collyridian heresy or Catholic Mariology.
  • Arabic has two words for "son":
    • Walad = biological son
    • Ibn = relational son (used 25x in the Quran for Jesus: "Isa ibn Maryam")
  • Surah 2:177 — a traveler is called ibn al-sabil ("son of the road") — no biological meaning
  • "Son of God" = he inherits everything the Father has = he IS God

III. "Where Does Jesus Say 'I Am God'?"

Take them to Matthew 26:62-66 (trial before Caiaphas):

  • Caiaphas asks: "Are you the Son of God? Are you the Messiah?"
  • Jesus says: "It is as you say" — Title 1: Son of God
  • "It is as you say" — Title 2: Messiah
  • "You will see the Son of Man coming in the clouds" (Daniel 7:14) — Title 3: Son of Man
  • Daniel 7:14: Son of Man is "from everlasting to everlasting, will have dominion over all tribes, nations, peoples and tongues" — that's God.
  • Caiaphas tore his robe: "This man has blasphemed!" — because he understood Jesus claimed divinity three times.
  • Muslims love "Son of Man" (25x in NT) thinking it means "just a man" — but Caiaphas's reaction proves it's a divine title.

Also: John 8:58 — "Before Abraham was, Yahweh" (I AM) — God's personal name from Exodus 3:14, used 6,823 times in the OT.

IV. "God Cannot Come to Earth"

  • God walked with Adam and Eve — Genesis 3:8-9
  • God wrestled Jacob — Genesis 32
  • Cloud by day, fire by night — Exodus 13
  • Burning bush — Exodus 3 and Surah 20:10-14
  • Surah 20:12: "Take off your shoes, you are on holy ground" — holy ground requires God's presence
  • Surah 20:14: "Truly I am Allah who is speaking to you" — God was in the bush, on earth, in 1400 BC
  • If God could come to earth then, why not 2,000 years ago?

V. The Crucifixion — Five Arguments Against Surah 4:157

  1. Internal contradiction: Surah 19:33 — Jesus says "Blessed be me the day I was born, the day I die, and the day I rise again." Surah 3:55 — God says to Jesus "mutawaffika" = "I will cause you to die" (Arabic translations changed this to "put to sleep" since the 1930s under government pressure)
  2. Historical record: Thallus & Phlegon (Greek, 52 AD), Tacitus (Roman), Josephus (Jewish) — all confirm Jesus died. No historian from the 1st-7th century denied it. Only Surah 4:157 from the 7th+ century denies it.
  3. Eyewitness testimony: John (3 years with Jesus), Mary (33 years as his mother), and the man on the cross himself: "Father, forgive them" (Luke 23:34), "Into your hands I commend my spirit" (Luke 23:46)
  4. The substitute problem: If Jesus let another die in his place, then appeared to the disciples claiming to be risen (John 20), showing his wounds to Thomas — that's a lying prophet.
  5. Moral argument: If Allah switched Jesus with another man and told no one for 600 years — that's deception. The Quran itself calls Allah deceitful: Surah 3:54, Surah 8:30, Surah 10:21, Surah 3:28.

PART 2: The Problem with the Quran

I. Islamic Claims vs. Evidence

  • Claim: The Quran is eternal (Surah 85:22 — preserved on tablets in heaven)
  • Problem: If tablets are co-eternal with God, that's shirk (associating something with God) — the very sin Muslims accuse Christians of. The Mu'tazilites raised this in the 10th century and were killed.
  • Claim: Revealed to Muhammad 610-632 AD
  • Problem: Muhammad didn't write it down. Arabic has only 28 letters (6 unique, 22 with dots). He had 22 years to learn to read/write but never did.
  • Claim: Compiled by Uthman in 652 AD, sent to 9 provinces
  • Problem: He burned all other manuscripts. Why burn them if they all agreed?
  • Claim: Written in the Quraishi dialect
  • Problem: Dialectical differences require diacritical marks (dots) and vowelization — these weren't invented until the 8th-9th century, 100-200 years later.

II. The Six Oldest Manuscripts

  1. Topkapi — Istanbul, Turkey
  2. Samarkand — Tashkent, Uzbekistan
  3. Ma'il — London, England
  4. Petropolitanus — Paris, France (St. Petersburg originally)
  5. Husseini — Cairo, Egypt
  6. Sana'a — Yemen (discovered 1975 in mosque dome trapdoor)

Muslim scholars Dr. Tayyar Altikulac and Dr. Ekmeleddin Ihsanoglu examined all six (2002-2007). Findings:

  • Not one is from the 7th century
  • Not one is complete
  • Not one agrees with the others
  • Not one fully agrees with today's Quran (the Hafs text)

III. The Modern Quran Is Only 94 Years Old

  • In 1924, Cairo had 37 different Arabic Qurans in circulation (not translations — original Arabic texts) with 60,000+ differences between them
  • Al-Azhar University chose the Hafs text — from a student named Hafs who died in 796 AD (144 years after Muhammad, from Kufa, not Mecca)
  • All other Arabic Qurans were thrown into the Nile River
  • 1936: Hafs text made standard across Egypt (King Farouk)
  • 1985: King Fahd of Saudi Arabia canonized the Hafs text worldwide — only ~40 years ago

IV. Manuscript Evidence

  • The Birmingham Folios (Surahs 18-20): Carbon dated 568-645 AD — before the Quran was finalized in 652
  • The Sana'a manuscript: Four labs (Oxford, Kiel, Lille, Zurich) date it 443-550 AD — before Muhammad was born
  • The stories in these folios (Moses at the burning bush, Seven Sleepers of Ephesus, Alexander the Great) are all borrowed from earlier sources — they predate the Quran
  • Over 4,000 variants found across the six major manuscripts: erasures, insertions, coverings, and text written over taped-over passages

V. Comparison with Biblical Manuscripts

  • Codex Sinaiticus (4th century) — complete NT, pristine, in the British Library
  • Codex Alexandrinus (5th century) — OT and NT, also in British Library
  • Codex Vaticanus (4th century) — in the Vatican
  • 365 NT manuscripts (partial or complete) exist from before the 6th century — all 300-400 years older than any Quranic manuscript
  • All we ask Muslims for: one complete, unchanged 7th-century Quran out of the nine that were supposedly sent. They cannot produce it.

  • All four schools of law (Hanbali, Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i) require execution of apostates
  • The only difference: Hanbali says immediately, Hanafi/Maliki say 3 days, Shafi'i gives 10 days
