Engaging Muslims: Defending Jesus and the Bible

Speaker: Jay Smith | Location: San Jose, CA

Core Thesis: Don't debate Islam unless you understand it deeply. Instead, defend what you know: Jesus is God, and the Bible is the Word of God. Muslims are eager to discuss both topics — use their questions as opportunities to present the gospel.

Four Cardinal Areas of Disagreement with Islam:

I. God Cannot Enter Time and Space (Islamic View)

Muslims believe Allah cannot come to earth without being corrupted. But our God has always entered time and space:

  • Walking with Adam and Eve in the Garden — Genesis 3
  • Wrestling with Jacob — Genesis 32
  • Pillar of cloud by day, fire by night — Exodus 13
  • The burning bush — Exodus 3

Quran bridge: Surah 20:10-14 — Moses sees the burning bush. The voice says "Take off your shoes, you are on holy ground" and "Truly I am Allah who is speaking to you." If God was in that bush in 1400 BC, why can't He come to earth 2,000 years ago?

II. The Trinity — Three Yet One

Muslims object: 1+1+1=3, not 1. Response: Try 1×1×1=1. But God isn't defined by math.

Key argument from the Quran itself:

  • Surah 1:1 — "In the name of the compassionate one, the merciful one"
  • Name #47 of Allah's 99 names: Al-Wadud — "the loving one"
  • Compassion, mercy, and love require an object. Who was Allah compassionate toward before creation?
  • In the Trinity, the Father has always loved the Son, the Son the Spirit — love existed eternally within the Godhead
  • We are relational because we're made in a relational God's image — Genesis 1:26-27

III. Jesus Died on the Cross and Rose Again

The Quran denies the crucifixion in only one verse: Surah 4:157 — "He did not die... another was given his image."

Five responses:

1. Contradiction within the Quran:

  • Surah 19:33 — Jesus himself says: "Blessed be me the day I was born, the day I die, and the day I rise again"
  • Surah 19:15 — John the Baptist says the same in third person
  • Surah 3:55 — God says to Jesus: "I will cause you to die" (Arabic mutawaffa = "cause to die," not "put to sleep" as some translations render it)

2. Theological problem — who can atone?

  • Surah 6:164 and Surah 53:38 — "No bearer of sins can take on the sins of another"
  • Agreed — no sinful person can. So who bears no sin?
  • Surah 19:19 — Angel Gabriel tells Mary: "I give you a righteous [sinless] son"
  • Surah 48:1-2 — Even Muhammad must ask forgiveness for sins. But not Jesus
  • Only the sinless one can bear our sins — Leviticus 17:11 (life is in the blood)

3. Historical evidence:

  • Greek: Thallus and Phlegon debating the darkness at Jesus' death (52 AD)
  • Roman: Tacitus records Jesus' death under Tiberius/Pontius Pilate (33 AD)
  • Jewish: Josephus records Jesus' death and the Christian belief in resurrection
  • All first/early second century — vs. one Quran verse from the 7th century

4. Eyewitness testimony:

  • John was at the foot of the cross — knew Jesus for 3 years
  • Mary was at the foot — knew her son for 33 years
  • The man on the cross said: "Father, forgive them" (Luke 23:34) and "Into your hands I commit my spirit" (Luke 23:46)

5. Moral problem:

  • If Allah put Jesus' image on another man, that's deception — contradicts Surah 6:164 and 53:38
  • God let a false religion grow for 600 years based on this event, then revealed the "truth" to an illiterate man in Arabia?
  • If Jesus let another die in his place, then appeared as the risen Christ to the disciples (John 20), that's a lying prophet

IV. Son of God — Not Biological

  • Arabic has two words: walad (biological son) and ibn (relational son)
  • The Quran uses ibn for Jesus 25 times — "Isa ibn Maryam"
  • Surah 2:177 — a traveler is called ibn al-sabil ("son of the road") — clearly not biological
  • "The Son of God" is a divine title meaning He inherits everything of the Father — He is God
  • Jesus claimed three divine titles before the Sanhedrin — Matthew 26:63-65: Son of God, Messiah, Son of Man
  • John 8:58 — "Before Abraham was, Yahweh [I AM]" — the personal name of God from Exodus 3:14
  • Yahweh appears 6,823 times in the Old Testament (vs. Adonai 340, Elohim 2,000+)
  • 1 John 5 — the entire chapter on believing Jesus is the Son of God

V. Why Atonement Is Necessary

  • Islam: good deeds vs. bad deeds on a scale — no assurance of salvation
  • The sirat bridge: razor-sharp over hell — Allah can throw anyone down at any time
  • Habakkuk 1:13 — God is so holy He cannot have sin in His presence
  • One sin expelled Adam and Eve from Eden
  • Genesis 3:15 — First prophecy: from Eve's line, a male will crush Satan's head (but Satan will bruise his heel — the crucifixion)
  • Isaiah 53 — The suffering servant
  • Isaiah 7:14 — A virgin will conceive, bear a son called Emmanuel ("God with us")
  • Surah 19:20 — The Quran confirms the virgin birth
  • Genesis 15 — God's covenant with Abraham: God alone walks through the sacrifice — He will shed His own blood
  • Surah 10:94 and 21:7 — The Quran tells Muslims: "If you have questions, go to the people of the book"

Key Contrasts:

  • Christians pray anytime, anywhere, in any language — Muslims pray 5 times daily, in Arabic, facing Mecca
  • Christians call God "Abba, Daddy" — Muslims are abdullahs (slaves) praying to a master
  • Christians have assurance of salvation — Muslims have none
  • Elohim (Hebrew) = three or more — Allah = monad (just one)

Practical Approach:

  • Build relationships with Muslims — have coffee, get to know them
  • Say: "I believe Jesus is God and the Bible is the Word of God. Do you have an opinion?"
  • Listen to their objections, respond with scripture
  • If you don't know an answer: "Give me a week" — then research and come back
  • Use the Quran as a bridge to the gospel — it contains enough to create dilemmas
  • Weave the gospel into every response
